Visa

Singapore is a great place to live as a web3/crypto person. One of the first things you'll need to consider is your visa status. Fortunately, Singapore offers a variety of visa options for those who want to live and work in the country.

The most common visa is the Employment Pass (EP), which is designed for skilled professionals and entrepreneurs. To be eligible for an EP, you'll need to have a job offer from a Singaporean company or be a founder of a startup in the country. Other visa options include the Personalised Employment Pass (PEP) and the EntrePass, which are designed for highly skilled individuals and entrepreneurs respectively.

Day-to-day life

Living in Singapore as a web3/crypto person is easy and convenient. The city-state is known for its high standard of living, excellent public transportation, and safe streets.

When it comes to day-to-day life, you'll find that Singapore has everything you need. From world-class restaurants and shopping malls to beautiful parks and hanging out with amazing people.

For me, Singapore is like a calm bubble where everyone is friendly and relaxed in a way I find really rare these days.

WEB3 community

Singapore has a thriving web3/crypto community, with a number of meetups and conferences taking place throughout the year. Attending these events is a great way to network with other web3/crypto professionals and stay up-to-date with the latest industry trends.

Some of the most popular web3/crypto events in Singapore include SGInnovate, the Singapore Fintech Festival, and the BlockShow Asia conference. Not to forget token2049 that in my opinion was the best last year.

Living costs

Singapore is known for being an expensive place to live, but as a web3/crypto person, you'll likely have a higher-than-average salary. And believe me, you get what you pay for. The cost of living in Singapore can vary greatly depending on your lifestyle and the neighborhood you choose to live in.

For example, if you choose to live in a luxury condominium in the heart of the city, you can expect to pay upwards of $5,000 SGD per month in rent. However, if you're willing to live a bit further out from the city center, you can find more affordable housing options. Or you can do like me and choose a coliving.
